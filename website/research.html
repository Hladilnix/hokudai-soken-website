Our Research

String Theory

Our group is heavily involved in the study of particle physics. We focus on the fundamental particles that make up the universe and the forces that they interact with. Our research includes the study of quarks, leptons, and the Higgs boson.

We aim to understand the underlying principles of string theory and how it can unify the different forces of nature. Our team collaborates with international researchers to push the boundaries of theoretical physics.

Particle Phenomenology

We also conduct research in quantum mechanics, focusing on the behavior of particles at the quantum level. Our research includes the study of quantum entanglement, superposition, and quantum tunneling.

Our phenomenology group works on developing models that can explain experimental results from particle accelerators and other high-energy physics experiments. We strive to bridge the gap between theory and experiment.

Cosmology

Our research in cosmology includes the study of the universe's origin, evolution, and eventual fate. We are particularly interested in the study of dark matter and dark energy, and how they influence the structure and expansion of the universe.

We use advanced simulations and observational data to explore the large-scale structure of the cosmos. Our goal is to uncover the mysteries of the universe and understand its fundamental nature.
